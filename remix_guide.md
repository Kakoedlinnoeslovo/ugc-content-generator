# UGC Remix Guide — Minor Variations

Creative remix patterns for generating subtle, scroll-stopping variations of
existing images. Each remix changes **only ONE axis** — the result should look
like a sibling photo from the same shoot, not a completely different image.

Every remix MUST feature a **completely different random person** — different
face, body type, age, gender, and ethnicity. No two remixes should share the
same person, and none should match the original. Describe each person briefly
in the prompt (e.g. "a tall bearded man in his 30s", "a teenage girl with
short bleached hair"). What matters is that the **scene, vibe, and
composition stay nearly identical** with one element swapped or tweaked.

Everything should feel like it was **filmed on an iPhone** — raw, slightly
off, Pinterest-strange, not polished editorial.

---

## Philosophy: One Change at a Time

Every remix mutates the source image along exactly ONE of these four axes:

| Axis | What changes | What stays the same |
|------|-------------|---------------------|
| **CLOTH** | The outfit / what the person wears | Pose, background, items, lighting |
| **ITEM** | An object the person holds or wears as accessory | Outfit, background, pose, lighting |
| **ITEM_ON_BG** | An object placed in the background/environment | Person, outfit, pose, lighting |
| **BACKGROUND** | The location / environment behind the person | Person, outfit, items, pose |

The change must be **visually dramatic and impossible to miss**. If you put
the original and remix side by side, a viewer should instantly spot the ONE
thing that changed — a completely different material/texture on clothing,
a huge impossible-to-ignore object, a massive background prop. The
composition and pose stay the same, but the swapped element must be BOLD.

---

## Axis 1: CLOTH — Dramatic Material / Texture Swaps

Swap the clothing to something made of a **completely different material or
texture** — the change must be visually LOUD and unmissable. Don't swap a
t-shirt for a sweater (boring). Swap a t-shirt for CHAINMAIL, ARMOR, TINFOIL,
a TRASH BAG, BUBBLE WRAP. The weirder the material, the better.

**IMPORTANT: The clothing swap must create a dramatic visual contrast with the
original. Think shocking material changes, not subtle style changes.**

**Item catalog (prioritize dramatic materials):**
- Full medieval chainmail shirt / hauberk (metal rings visible, heavy, draped)
- Knight's armor breastplate + gauntlets worn over casual pants
- Tinfoil / aluminum foil wrapped as a full outfit — dress, top, or bodysuit
- Trash bag dress — black garbage bags cut and worn as a dress or poncho
- Bubble wrap bodysuit or vest (air pockets clearly visible)
- Full-body hazmat suit (yellow, with hood down around neck)
- Leather motorcycle armor / padded racing suit
- Astronaut suit (white, bulky, helmet off or under arm)
- Surgical scrubs + latex gloves + face mask pulled down to chin
- Wetsuit (black neoprene, full body, zipper visible)
- Firefighter turnout coat (reflective yellow stripes, heavy)
- Full fur coat (massive, floor-length, dramatic) over underwear only
- Vinyl / PVC full-body catsuit (shiny, skin-tight, red or black)
- Cardboard box armor — DIY medieval armor made from shipping boxes
- Cling film / plastic wrap dress (transparent, layered, strange)
- Life jacket (bright orange) worn as the only top

**Example remix instructions:**
- "Same scene, same pose, same background. Replace entire outfit with a full
  medieval chainmail shirt — heavy metal rings clearly visible, draped over
  the body, paired with the same casual pants/shoes. iPhone snapshot, direct
  flash, candid."
- "Person now wears a full-body wetsuit — black neoprene, zipper down the
  back, tight-fitting. Same background, same pose, same lighting. Raw phone
  photo feel."
- "Swap clothing for a DIY cardboard box armor — shipping boxes cut into
  breastplate and arm guards, held together with packing tape, worn over
  a plain t-shirt. Same location, same exact pose, same iPhone flash
  aesthetic. The cardboard should be clearly visible and dramatic."

---

## Axis 2: ITEM — Bold Held Objects & Accessories

Add or swap ONE object the person holds, wears, or interacts with. The item
must be **clearly visible and eye-catching** — big enough to register
instantly in the frame. No subtle accessories that blend in.

**IMPORTANT: The item should be large, strange, and immediately noticeable.
A small ring = BAD. A giant sunflower covering half the torso = GOOD.
Weird glasses must be DRAMATIC — oversized, bizarre shape, impossible to miss.**

**Item catalog (prioritize large / eye-catching):**
- Giant single flower (bigger than head): oversized sunflower, giant protea,
  huge dahlia, massive calla lily bouquet filling both arms
- HUGE bouquet of wildflowers — so large it covers the person's entire chest
- Inflatable held at body level: pink flamingo (full pool-size), giant donut,
  oversized banana (1 meter long)
- DRAMATIC unusual glasses: oversized Y2K shield visor (covers half the face),
  huge heart-shaped frames (neon pink, filling the face), welding goggles
  (industrial, dark, worn on face), swim goggles on forehead, giant
  bedazzled cat-eye sunglasses, ski goggles, novelty-sized round frames
- Knight's full helmet WORN on head (visor up or down) — shiny metal,
  medieval, dramatic
- Fencing sword / épée held upright at side like a walking cane
- Whole raw fish (large salmon or tuna) held in both arms like a baby
- Vintage rotary phone (bright red or cream) held to ear, coiled cord visible
- Massive bouquet of red balloons (10+ balloons on strings) held overhead
- Baguette (full French baguette, 60cm+) tucked under arm
- Giant rubber duck (50cm+, bright yellow) clutched to chest
- Chainsaw (large, orange, held casually at hip)
- Disco ball (basketball-sized) held in both hands at chest level
- Full-size guitar / bass held vertically
- Fire extinguisher held casually like a handbag
- Taxidermy bird (large parrot or owl) perched on shoulder
- Toilet plunger held upright like a scepter

**Example remix instructions:**
- "Same photo, same outfit, same background. Person now holds a GIANT
  sunflower (head of flower bigger than their face) at chest height, stem
  in both hands. The flower must be prominent and fill a large part of the
  frame. Same iPhone flash aesthetic."
- "Add oversized Y2K shield visor sunglasses covering the top half of
  the person's face — huge, wraparound, reflective chrome lenses. Nothing
  else changes — same outfit, same background, same phone flash, same pose.
  The glasses should be the most noticeable element."
- "Person now holds a whole raw salmon (large, 60cm+) cradled in both
  arms like a baby. The fish should be clearly visible and fill a significant
  portion of the frame. Same location, clothes, lighting. iPhone snapshot."

## Axis 3: ITEM_ON_BG — MASSIVE Background Object Placement

Place ONE unexpected object into the background. The object must be **LARGE
enough to dominate a significant portion of the frame** — at minimum 30-40%
of the background area. No small props tucked into corners. The object should
be so big and out-of-place that it's the FIRST thing a viewer notices.

**IMPORTANT: The object must be HUGE, scene-dominating, impossible to miss.
A tiny teddy bear in the corner = BAD. A human-sized teddy bear filling
half the background = GOOD.**

**Item catalog (all items must be OVERSIZED / scene-filling):**
- Giant inflatable pink flower (3+ meters tall, Jeff Koons scale, filling
  the entire background behind the person)
- MASSIVE pile of oranges / lemons / watermelons — hundreds of them, covering
  the entire ground behind the person like a carpet of fruit
- Human-sized teddy bear (taller than the person) standing or sitting
  directly behind them, filling the frame
- Wall of stacked CRT televisions (floor to ceiling, all showing static)
- Giant inflatable flamingo (pool-sized, 2+ meters) placed on dry land
  behind the person
- Wrecking ball / giant chrome sphere hanging from above, filling upper frame
- Full-size horse (real or statue) standing directly behind the person
- Mountain of shoes — hundreds of pairs piled waist-high behind them
- Giant neon sign covering the entire wall ("MAYBE" or "YES" or "EXIT"
  in massive glowing letters)
- Full decorated Christmas tree (2+ meters) directly behind person in
  a clearly non-Christmas setting (beach, parking lot, bathroom)
- Huge pile of flowers — floor covered in thousands of loose petals and
  stems, knee-deep, surrounding the person
- Giant disco ball (1+ meter diameter) hanging just above head height
- Stack of 20+ pizza boxes forming a tower as tall as the person
- Car (actual full car) parked directly behind person in an indoor setting
- Kiddie pool (large, inflatable, filled with water) taking up the
  entire floor area behind the person
- Giant banana sculpture (2+ meters, bright yellow) standing upright

**Example remix instructions:**
- "Same photo exactly. Place a MASSIVE inflatable pink flower sculpture
  (3 meters tall, bright pink petals, yellow center) directly behind the
  person, filling most of the background. The flower should be impossible
  to miss — it dominates the frame. Same iPhone snapshot quality."
- "Add a giant teddy bear TALLER than the person, standing directly behind
  them and filling the entire right half of the background. Brown fur,
  classic teddy bear shape, absurdly oversized. Person unchanged — same
  outfit, pose, lighting. iPhone flash photo."
- "Cover the entire ground behind the person with a MASSIVE pile of loose
  flower petals and stems — knee-deep, thousands of flowers, covering every
  inch of visible floor. Same person, same outfit, same pose. Raw phone
  camera aesthetic."

## Axis 4: BACKGROUND — Scene Swap

Change the location / environment while keeping the person, their outfit,
and their pose identical. The new background should feel mundane-weird,
like an iPhone photo taken in an unexpected place.

**Location catalog:**
- Laundromat (fluorescent lights, washing machines, linoleum floor)
- Empty parking garage (concrete, harsh overhead lights, painted lines)
- Gas station at night (neon glow, pumps, wet asphalt)
- Public bathroom / restroom (tile walls, mirrors, harsh light)
- Grocery store aisle (shelves of cereal / canned goods)
- Hotel hallway (patterned carpet, numbered doors, recessed lighting)
- Elevator interior (mirrored walls, buttons, overhead panel light)
- Rooftop with AC units and pipes (urban, industrial)
- Stairwell (concrete, handrail, emergency exit sign)
- Fast food restaurant booth (vinyl seats, tray of food on table)
- Hospital waiting room (plastic chairs, fluorescent lights)
- Empty swimming pool (drained, blue tiles, no water)
- Construction site (orange barriers, scaffolding, hard hats)
- Car interior / backseat (dashboard lights, leather seats)
- School hallway (lockers, linoleum floor, fluorescent tubes)
- Subway platform (tiles, yellow line, tunnel visible)
- Garden center / plant nursery (rows of potted plants, greenhouse)
- Thrift store interior (racks of clothes, cluttered shelves)
- Cemetery (headstones, grass, overcast sky)
- Abandoned building interior (peeling paint, broken windows, debris)

**Example remix instructions:**
- "Same person, same outfit, same pose. Move to a laundromat background —
  row of washing machines, fluorescent overhead lights, linoleum floor.
  iPhone flash photo, same raw snapshot quality."
- "Transplant to a gas station at night — neon glow, wet asphalt, fuel pumps.
  Same clothing, same casual pose, phone-camera flash aesthetic."
- "Change background to an empty drained swimming pool — blue tiles, no water,
  overcast sky. Person unchanged. iPhone candid snapshot."

## Strange UGC Combinations (Multi-Axis for GPT Inspiration)

When GPT generates ideas from a gallery image, it can combine elements across
axes for extra weirdness — but each individual remix should still feel like a
**minor tweak**, not a total transformation. These are inspiration combos:

- Chainmail armor + holding calla lilies + laundromat background
- White underpants + swim goggles on forehead + grocery store aisle
- Fur coat over bare legs + raw fish in arms + parking garage

## iPhone / Phone Camera Aesthetic Rules

Every remix MUST maintain this lo-fi phone-shot quality:
- Direct on-camera flash (harsh, flat, blown-out highlights, hard shadow on wall)
- Slight overexposure on skin
- Visible digital noise / grain
- Off-center or slightly crooked framing
- Mundane, un-styled environment
- Subject looks like a real person — not a model on set
- No studio lighting, no softboxes, no beauty dish
- Color temperature slightly off (too warm, too cool, greenish fluorescent)
- The vibe is "my friend took this on their phone" not "fashion shoot"

---

## Key Rules for Remix Generation

1. **ONE change, but make it BOLD** — the remix changes exactly ONE axis,
   but that change must be visually dramatic and impossible to miss. A viewer
   glancing at original vs remix for 1 second must instantly spot the difference.
2. **No subtle swaps** — a pink sweater instead of a white t-shirt = BAD.
   A chainmail hauberk instead of a white t-shirt = GOOD. A tiny bear in the
   corner = BAD. A bear taller than the person filling the background = GOOD.
3. **Different random person in every remix** — each remix MUST feature a
   completely different person (face, body type, age, gender, ethnicity).
   Describe them briefly in the prompt. No two remixes share the same person.
4. **iPhone aesthetic mandatory** — every remix must look like a phone snapshot.
   Raw, imperfect, harsh flash, digital noise, candid energy.
5. **Strange > pretty** — lean into Pinterest-weird, Tumblr-strange, oddly
   specific combinations. Shocking materials, absurd scale, unexpected textures.
6. **Self-contained prompt** — each remix instruction must describe the FULL
   final image, not just the delta from the original.
7. **Specify size and material** — for CLOTH: name the exact material (metal
   rings, aluminum foil, black plastic, neoprene). For ITEM_ON_BG: specify
   size relative to the person ("taller than the person", "filling the wall").
8. **3-5 sentences** — keep remix instructions concise but specific about the
   change, the preserved elements, and the iPhone camera aesthetic.
